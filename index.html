Chenyang Wang

Lecturer in Econometrics

Current teaching

Postgraduate modules in Introduction to Econometrics and Further Econometrics.

Biography

I completed my PhD in Economics at the University of Birmingham (UK) under the supervision of Professor Alessandra Guariglia. After completing my PhD in 2021 I worked as a Lecturer in Economics at the University of Reading before joining the University of Manchester as a Teaching Associate in December 2022. Since September 2024, I have been a Lecturer in Econometrics at the University of Manchester, where I primarily teach postgraduate econometrics. I love to teach quantiative modules including statistics and econometrics, especially use clear and accessible language to explain complex ideas to help students build strong analytical foundations.

Resarch interests

My research mainly focuses on the following areas:

*Liquidity constraints and entrepreneurship in China and the UK, with particular interest in how financial frictions shape entrepreneurial decisions and outcomes.

*Investor sentiment and stock market dynamics in China, examining how behavioural factors influence market performance.

*Enhancing the formation of heterogeneous study groups among undergraduate and postgraduate students, with the aim of improving cross‑national collaboration and learning (Education Scholarship).

*Supporting UG students in selecting optional modules, focusing on improving the decision‑making process and student experience (Education Scholarship).
